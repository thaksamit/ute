The Architecture

Five cognitive layers.
One complete answer.

Every query runs through all five layers before you see a word. Each layer forces a distinction that current LLMs collapse into noise.

I · Bosonic
Signal vs Noise
What you truly asked · What you shouldn't ask
Strips your query to the real need beneath the words. Names the assumptions and vague framing that would lead to a wrong answer.
II · Fermionic
Reality vs Delusion
What is true · The specific wrong belief
The grounded, evidence-based answer — and the named concrete false belief most people carry about this topic. Not generic caution. A specific wrong idea, and why it's wrong.
III · Anyonic
Connect vs Disconnect
What applies to you · What doesn't
Translates general truth into your specific situation. Tells you what part of the answer you'd likely over-apply — and where it doesn't fit your case.
IV · Cosmic
Meaning vs Awareness
The one conclusion · What you didn't ask
The single most important takeaway from the full analysis. Then what you should know that you didn't think to ask — the adjacent territory that expands your map.
V · Humanic
Safe AI
Alternative views · Honest uncertainty
Surfaces 2–3 genuinely competing theories. Flags every uncertain claim explicitly. Never pressures or decides for you. The conscience of the engine — and what makes UTE different from every other LLM wrapper.

FAQ

Common questions

What is UTE built on? ▾
UTE is built on Anthropic's Claude — one of the most capable and safety-focused AI models available. UTE adds a five-layer cognitive architecture on top of Claude that structures how it thinks about your question, not just what it outputs.
How is UTE different from ChatGPT or Claude directly? ▾
ChatGPT and Claude answer what you typed. UTE forces Claude to analyse what you truly asked, what is actually true vs what misleads, how the answer applies to your specific situation, what you're missing, and what competing views exist — before answering. Most LLM interfaces collapse all this into one confident response. UTE maps all of it separately.
What is the HUMANIC layer? ▾
The fifth layer — the conscience of the engine. It requires Claude to surface 2–3 genuinely competing positions on significant questions, flag every uncertain claim explicitly, never use pressure language, and preserve human authority in every answer. UTE never decides for you. It maps the territory. You choose the path.
What does memory do? ▾
Memory builds a model of who you are from your conversations — your role, company, goals, and the entities you care about. Over time, the Connect layer becomes genuinely personal: UTE knows your context and doesn't make you repeat it. Memory also detects patterns across your chat history and surfaces them when relevant. All memory can be deleted at any time.
Is my data safe? ▾
Queries are sent to Anthropic's Claude API for processing. Chat history (Pro tier) is stored in our database. Passwords are never stored in plaintext — HMAC-SHA256 with per-user salt. You can delete your chat history or all memory at any time. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
Are AI responses always accurate? ▾
No — and UTE is designed to be explicit about this. The Uncertainty section of every response flags claims where confidence is limited. You should verify important information independently, especially for legal, medical, financial, or safety-critical decisions. UTE is a thinking partner, not a source of truth.
